Projects

  • WWU Parking App

    UX/UI Design

    2025

    Park Western: WWU Mobile App

    A mobile parking application for Western Washington University, giving WWU commuters a better understanding of parking avilability on campus.



    Role:

    Lead Designer



    Technologies:

    Figma.



    Users:

    WWU Students, Guests, and Faculty.



    Problem:

    Finding reliable parking on campus during peak school hours is an issue that plagues WWU students and guests alike. Knowing which lots are already full and which have spaces still available can be a struggle. Which can result in unnecessary lateness or tardiness to events and classes on campus.



    Solution:

    Through the use of newly installed parking lot cameras with image detection software, and a new mobile parking application for Western Washington University, these problems can be alleviated. The proposed technology relies upon cameras that track available parking lot spaces and also provide users with a live feed of parking lots. Giving them a better visual indicator, along with metric data, of how crowded certain lots ares. The mobile parking application gives users a live camera feed and number of available parking spaces for each parking lot on campus . These parking lots are sorted between student, guest, and faculty parking, so the experience is tailored to each of these user groups.





    WIREFRAMES

    The design was created initially using a wireframe to develop a low-fidelity mockup of the necessary features, pages, navigation, and components for the next design iteration. In this wireframe, the homepage, parking, and map pages were planned out, creating a flow between the main homepage and to the subpages of the system. The notice page was created later, after the inital wireframes.

    RESEARCH


    As the application is tied to the existing brand entity of Western Washington University, I needed to ensure that the design looked and felt like it belonged to WWU's trademark by following in cohesive design. After some research of their websites and designs, I identified specific colors (light green, blues, white) and fonts (montserrat, Fira Sans) for use in the application. 

    HOME SCREEN

    The home screen promptly greets users with the number of available spots and lots. Immediately providing them with relevant data, if a user only wants to take a quick glance at the application. For users wanting to look further, they have three simple navigation options. The home screen functions as a hub to store the crux of the app's information and routes all navigation to the various subpages. 

    PARKING SCREEN

    The first option for navigation is to look at parking availability across lots on WWU's campus. These parking lots are sorted into student, faculty, and guest parking, the three user groups for the application. By default, student parking is shown first. Each relevant parking lot for the current user group is shown as a "card" with information on the current lot, including live camera feeds. Users have the ability to star lots, which will then show up first in the list of parking lots, allowing users to easily access their preferred lots faster. Furthermore, users can select "park now" which will pull up the relevant lot information in Park Mobile. The university currently uses Park Mobile as their pay-to-park application. So, our application allows users to seamlessly check availability, and then park on demand. This reduces the overhead work that WWU would need to put forth in order to develop the application. 

    PARKING MAP

    The parking map allows for users to search all parking lots on campus through a bird's eye view. Giving users a better sense of where unfamiliar lots are located, especially for those who are new to campus or visitors.  Users can use their fingers to pinch and scroll through the map. Parking lots are sorted by student, guest, and faculty parking. Lots not available for the current user group are grayed out, while those that are available, can be clicked on to show more information, such as name, lot size, and designation. Selecting a lot also allows a user to elect to "park now" which pulls up the relevant parking information in Park Mobile (further discussed in the above section).

    PARKING NOTICES

    The parking notices page is used to deliver university issued notices related to parking. Notices are sorted by month and year, from most recent notices to oldest. Additionally, notices marked in red are ongoing, while green marked notices have been completed, allowing for users to see past notices while keeping updated with current ones. Clicking the 'read more' button will link users to the university webpage with the official notice.

    FINAL DESIGNS

    The design of the mobile application emphasizes sleek simplistic design, easy navigation, color cohesion, brand identity, and readability. Additionally, the application design responds to the problem of finding parking availability on WWU's campus, with the most minimally invasive and easy to implement design.


  • Covid Memories

    Web Development

    2024

    Covid Memories Audio Library

    Covid memories is an audio library that was created to share the stories of people who experienced the COVID-19 pandemic.



    Project Type:

    4 Member Group



    Duration:

    One Academic Year



    Languages:

    HTML, CSS, Javascript



    Technologies:

    MongoDB, ExpressJS, NodeJS





    Our client wanted to have an interactive web application for hosting a collection of interviews with various individuals, reflecting on the impact of the pandemic. Additionally, the client wanted the ability to add new interviews to the collection and edit existing information by authorized users. Allowing for people outside of the project to interact with the audio collection but not change any internal information. Eventually, the client hoped that this project would expand to host other types of audio collections like museum databases.


    Our group achieved this by creating a website that allows authorized users to log in, upload their own audio entries, and fill in the corresponding metadata describing the file. The files metadata includes different information like the track name, speaker’s name, themes, as well as a description of what was said during the track. To add intractability, as users listen through the main playlist, they are given the option to choose different branching points of audio, like choosing your own adventure. This spawns the chosen playlist into the main playlist. Additionally, users can move away from the main playlist, and listen to other playlists of audio, which are grouped together by themes, like coping, which tells the story of how different people coped during the pandemic.

    SECOND DESIGN

    THIRD DESIGN


    During our project development I took on the role of designing our front-end design and adding website functionality. As our project spanned a full year of development, each new academic quarter brought an opportunity to reiterate the design. Our team worked using Agile flow, allowing for iterative development of our application across quarters. Each quarter, I would receive feedback on my design from my project teammates and our client and then use this feedback to fuel a new and improved version of the design.   • Alcohol Cessation Application

    Android Application

    2024

    Alcohol Cessation Application

    Android mobile application aimed at college students, allowing them to track drinking related data to get a better understanding of the impact of their drinking choices.



    Project Type:

    3 Member Group



    Duration:

    One Quarter



    Languages:

    Java, XML



    Technologies:

    Android Studio





    Our application addresses the issue of alcohol overconsumption in American college students, where binge drinking rates consistently range between 30% to 40% for student populations, as reported by the National Library of Medicine. Binge drinking is a harmful practice, and our application seeks to visualize the harmful effects of overdrinking practices.


    Taking into account these issues, we designed an application that allows for students to log their alcohol consumption on a daily, weekly, or monthly basis. Logging their drink details and time of consumption. The system uses this input to provide blood alcohol content (BAC), calories intake, units consumed, and money spent displayed across daily, weekly, and monthly intervals. Allowing for users to track drinking related data to get a better understanding of the impact of their drinking choices and develop healither habits.


    INITIAL DESIGN MOCKUPS

    For the purposes of our application design, the group split our responsibilities between the different scenes. I was responsible for creating the initial design mockups for the home screen and the logging screens. My initial designs were created using Canva and placed an emphasis on proving the user with their data and pushing them to improve their habits by supplying them with tips and goals. Furthermore, I tried to achieve this without overwhelming the user with too many choices or UI elements.

    LOGGING SCREEN

    The logging screens provide users with the option to view their data in daily, weekly, or monthly view for the current time period. For daily and weekly views, see their logged data for the day displayed on a line chart. For monthly view, it is displayed in a color coded chart. Each of these charts display four different metrics of data– units consumed, calories consumed, BAC levels, and money spent. For each of these metrics, a total is displayed at the top, which shows the sum of entries for a particular metric for the selected time period. 

    NAVIGATION SCREENS

    In the daily summary users are greeted with the date and time, reminding them of the information that will be used when logging drinks. Additionally, they are given the option to add or update their personal information and to track their current goals or make new ones. In the reporting screen, users are given an option to decide which view they would like to see. This simplified the original prototype design by eliminating too many buttons on the graph pages themselves.

  • Memorable Password Manager

    Desktop Application

    2024

    Memorable Password Manager

    Customizable passphrase generator and manager, which allows users to design passphrases according to their personal preferences and security needs.



    Project Type:

    3 Member Group



    Duration:

    One Quarter



    Languages:

    C#



    Technologies:

    Windows Forms





    Creating a strong and memorable password is a crucial challenge for many people. Highly secure passwords are often difficult to remember, which can lead individuals to use less secure but more easily remembered options. This compromise increases the risk of unauthorized access to personal and sensitive information. Our project addresses this issue by developing a passphrase generator designed to balance security and memorability.


    The findings from our survey of students at Western Washington University show that a majority of the participants, have difficulty remembering their passwords or have trouble logging in to their accounts. The majority of users also prefer the memorablity of their passphrases, even at the expense of its security. Which is why they also favor memorable passphrases over highly secure random strings. This highlights the need for a tool that can help people create, remember, and store secure passwords easily.


    INITIAL MOCKUPS


    The design of our application is focused on providing users with broad customization options for creating secure and memorable passwords and phrases. Before developing our desktop application, I created our teams initial design prototype using Canva, an online graphic design tool. This allowed our team to foster a better understanding of the layout of the application, and which features we wanted to implement in our project.

    INITIAL FORM DESIGN



    FINAL DESIGN


    In the implementation of our project and final design, our team decided to develop our application using Windows Forms, a UI framework for building desktop applications. To make the design more visually appealing and polished, we imported a Guna UI/UX Framework for creating our controls and components.
